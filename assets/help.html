GroupStreamer

GroupStreamer is a music recommender system for parties and gatherings: it picks out the best music on your Android device given the people around you.

This is a short tutorial on how to use it. Enjoy !

Basic scenario

Imagine you are hosting a party. You and hopefully a few of your guests have the GroupStreamer app on their phone. One of you will create a group for the party, and all of you will join the group. You will then decide on one person that is providing the music—i.e. the DJ. This person will plug his phone into loudspeakers and start playing music through GroupStreamer.

GroupStreamer tries to select the music according to the preferences of everyone in the group. People's musical tastes are automatically inferred from the contents of their music library.

The groups list

This screen shows you the groups that are nearby. Often, you'll first want to create a new group. You can then ask your friends / guests to join the group you've just created simply by touching its name.

The music player

The main screen is the music player. If you want to provide the music for the group, you can grab the DJ spot (it's first come, first served). You'll be then presented with a standard media player bar.

If you are not the DJ, you can still see which song is currently playing. You can also give a rating on the current song, which will help GroupStreamer make more precise recommendations in the future.
